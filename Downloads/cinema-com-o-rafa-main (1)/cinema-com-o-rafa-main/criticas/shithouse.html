English Review

Good evening, my dear followers. I just watched Shithouse, directed by Cooper Raif, and I wanted to share my initial impressions or thoughts on the film, which was my second time watching. It's very special when you see certain themes in a movie that can be related to your own life. And man, this film kind of resonates with my personal life. I'm a 19-year-old guy, and Rafael is a dreamer, much like me.

I think it's not in the context, but I go through the same situations as the film deals with regarding college, specifically. About dynamics of friendships, parties, loneliness – it's a pretty cool theme that Cooper Raif brings up, and I believe it's extremely important to have films like these. Light-hearted films with an interesting purpose that can offer new perspectives to the generations going through these experiences now.

What's interesting is that one of my best friends now, Caden, and I connected because he liked my comment on Letterboxd about this film. It was a simple review, and he found it cool, so we started talking. If it weren't for this movie, I wouldn't have the best friendships of my life today. The film is specifically about that, but for me, it's something different. It hits me differently because some dilemmas the character Alex goes through, I feel like I've been through them sometimes, and I might go through them in college, you know? Like not finding a group of friends, not being invited to things, experiencing that loneliness, which is normal these days.

I tell you, it's increasingly difficult to find someone, whether it's a boyfriend, girlfriend, or just friends because everyone has their own purposes. As he says in the film, college is a somewhat selfish time. I think it was Maggie who said that, the character played by Dylan Gelula. So, it's in these moments that you start to get to know a person, and sometimes you lose that altruistic side of knowing how the other person is doing, and you just think about yourself. I don't think it's entirely wrong, but nowadays, everyone is focused on finding people who are strictly like themselves, and sometimes it's hard to embrace everyone.

I can speak for the people in my college – I live with various people with different styles, different tastes, and yet I get along fine with my friends. I don't need to be the known guy in college, far from it. I want loyal friendships that like me for who I am, you know? And I think this film brings that in a cute, interesting way, like everything will be okay, even with these adversities, this loneliness, being away from family. He has a very focused vision, like, if everything goes wrong, I'll go back to my family. I'm repeating it, but there will be a correct moment, you know? There are friendships, relationships, regardless of anything, that will happen at the right time. Because, I speak from personal experience, until halfway through this year, I had good friends I knew since high school, and I wasn't so close to everyone in college. But after a while, for some reasons, I started becoming friends with new people, and that made me feel so good.

It gives me motivation to go to college, and before, I felt like, man, nobody wants to be my friend, nobody wants to talk to me, how am I going to approach someone? And after that happened naturally, man, things got much better for me, you know? I feel that it's very interesting, man, a guy in his twenties, Cooper Raif, who wrote such a film. It's beautiful to find your new passions. My dream is to find someone to love, to date, you know? And I feel that this person will come at the right moment. I find this process that he shows, this natural love, where you have a great night, and the next day it's not so great with the person, so beautiful. But what Shithouse

represents for Rafa is hope. That you will find new people, that everything will be okay. I feel that it's a film of extreme importance for people of our age; I'm only nineteen, going through these internal conflicts, insecurities. And this film deals well with that. Shithouse is a really cool film, and I appreciate every time I watch it. It's truly one of my favorites – films like this, like Whiplash, La La Land,Fantastic Mr. Fox, where I have a vision, man, everything will be okay. I'm just so thankful, and this review is being done at 3:49 AM. I was so thoughtful after the film, and man, I'm so happy.

I took a lot of vacations, and I'm going back to college on December 5.
